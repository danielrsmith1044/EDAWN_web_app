1   Getting Started

Registering Your Account

You will receive a unique invite link from the EDAWN BRE team. Open that link in any web browser — it will take you directly to the registration page with your invite code pre-filled. Choose a username and password, then click Create Account. You only need to register once.

Note: The invite link is single-use. If you share it with someone else and they register first, you will need to request a new link from staff.

Logging In

Go to the portal URL provided by EDAWN and click Log In. Enter your username and password. If you forget your password, click Forgot password? on the login page and follow the instructions, or contact staff to have a temporary password issued.

Completing Your Training

Until your training is marked complete by staff, a banner on your dashboard will display a link to book your training session via Calendly. Once you have completed training, staff will update your account and the banner will disappear.

Tip: Complete your training session before you start contacting companies. It covers what to say, what to look for during a visit, and how to record your findings in the portal.

3   Your Assigned Companies

Click My Companies in the sidebar to see all companies assigned to you. By default the list shows your active assignments — companies you still need to visit. Use the filter tabs to view completed or lost assignments as well.

Company Detail Page

Click a company name to open its detail page. This page shows:

  • The company's name, industry, address, and website
  • Your primary contact name and phone number
  • A history of all contact attempts and visits you have logged
  • Buttons to log a new contact attempt or record a completed visit

Assignment Status

StatusMeaning
ActiveAssigned to you — outreach is in progress
CompletedYou successfully visited this company
LostThree contact attempts were made with no response
Note: A company is automatically marked Lost after you log three contact attempts without a completed visit. This is by design — it frees the company up for re-assignment and keeps your list focused.

4   Logging a Contact Attempt

A contact attempt is any outreach you make to a company that does not result in a completed in-person visit. Log one each time you call, email, or stop by and do not connect with anyone.

How to Log a Contact Attempt

  • Open the company detail page from My Companies
  • Click Log Contact Attempt
  • Select the method: Phone, Email, or In-Person
  • Add any notes about the attempt (optional but helpful)
  • Click Save
Tip: Log a contact attempt even when you leave a voicemail or send an email with no reply. Accurate records help staff understand which companies may need a different approach.
Important: After your third contact attempt on a company, the assignment will automatically be marked Lost. Make sure your first two attempts are genuine — do not log them all at once just to clear a company from your list.

5   Logging a Completed Visit

A visit is an in-person meeting with someone at the company where you were able to have a meaningful conversation. This is the primary goal of the program.

How to Log a Visit

  • Open the company detail page from My Companies
  • Click Log Visit
  • Fill in the visit details — what you discussed, any concerns or expansions signals the business shared, and how the overall visit went
  • Click Save

Once saved, the assignment is automatically marked Completed and the company is updated to Visited. The EDAWN staff team will be notified automatically.

Note: You can edit a visit note after saving it if you need to add or correct information. Open the company detail page and click the edit icon next to the visit entry.

What to Include in Your Visit Notes

FieldWhat to Capture
General notesHow the meeting went, who you spoke with, overall sentiment
Expansion signalsAny mention of hiring, growth, new locations, or capital investment
Concerns / challengesWorkforce issues, regulatory concerns, anything EDAWN can help with
Follow-up neededAnything that requires a response or referral from EDAWN staff
Tip: Fill in your visit notes as soon as possible after the meeting while details are fresh. Even brief notes are more useful than none.

6   Browsing & Requesting Companies

If you are ready to take on more work, you can browse unassigned companies and request to be assigned one.

How to Browse Companies

  • Click Browse Companies in the sidebar
  • The list shows all companies not currently assigned to any volunteer
  • Filter by industry to find companies that match your background or network
  • Click Request Assignment on any company you would like to take on

Request Rules

RuleDetail
Pending request limitYou may have up to 3 pending requests at one time
Active assignment limitNon-BBV volunteers may only hold 1 active assignment at a time. Complete or lose your current company before requesting another (unless you are BBV certified).
Approval requiredRequests are reviewed and approved by EDAWN staff — assignment is not immediate
CompetitionMultiple volunteers can request the same company. When staff approves one, all other requests for that company are automatically denied.
BBV Certification: Once you have visited at least one company in each of three consecutive calendar months, you become eligible for BBV (Business Builder Volunteer) certification. BBV-certified volunteers can hold more than one active assignment at a time. Ask staff for details.

7   Badges & the Leaderboard

The portal tracks your activity and automatically awards badges when you hit milestones. Badges are a way to recognize your contributions and show your progress over time.

How Badges Are Earned

Most badges are awarded automatically when you reach a threshold — for example, completing your first visit, or completing five visits. Some badges are awarded manually by EDAWN staff for special achievements. You will see a notification on your dashboard when a new badge is awarded.

Click Badges in the sidebar to see all available badges, which ones you have earned, and which ones you are working toward.

The Leaderboard

The Leaderboard page ranks all volunteers by the number of completed visits. It is updated in real time as visits are logged. Use it to see where you stand and to celebrate the contributions of your fellow volunteers.

Tip: Badges and leaderboard rankings are based on completed visits, not contact attempts. The best way to climb the leaderboard is to convert your outreach into actual in-person meetings.

8   Messages

The Messages section is a shared communication board for all volunteers and EDAWN staff.

Group Messages

Group messages are visible to all volunteers. Staff may use this board to share program updates, tips, or recognition. You can post your own messages as well — ask questions, share experiences, or encourage fellow volunteers.

Private Messages

If you need to reach EDAWN staff directly — for example, to flag a company concern or ask a question you would rather not post publicly — select Private message to staff when composing a new message. Only you and staff will be able to see it.

Replies

You can reply to any message thread. Click the message title to open the thread and add your reply at the bottom.

9   Resources

The Resources page contains reference materials curated by the EDAWN team — links to documents, guides, and tools that are useful for your outreach work. Materials are organized by category. Click any title to open the linked resource.

Resources are maintained by staff. If you think something useful is missing, send a message or mention it to your EDAWN contact.
