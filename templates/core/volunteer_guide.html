1. Getting Started

Registering Your Account

You will receive a unique invite link from the EDAWN BRE team. Open that link in any web browser; it will take you directly to the registration page with your invite code pre-filled. Choose a username and password, then click Create Account. You only need to register once.

Note: The invite link is single-use. If you share it with someone else and they register first, you will need to request a new link from staff.

Logging In

Go to the portal URL provided by EDAWN and click Log In. Enter your username and password. If you forget your password, click "Forgot password?" on the login page and follow the instructions, or contact staff to have a temporary password issued.

Completing Your Training

Until your training is marked complete by staff, a banner on your dashboard will display a link to book your training session via Calendly. Once you have completed training, staff will update your account and the banner will disappear.

Tip: Complete your training session before you start contacting companies. It covers what to say, what to look for during a visit, and how to record your findings in the portal.

2. Your Dashboard

The dashboard is the first page you see after logging in. It gives you a quick overview of everything that matters to you.

Use the sidebar on the left to navigate to any section of the portal. On a phone or small screen, tap the menu icon at the top to expand the navigation.

3. Your Assigned Companies

Click My Companies in the sidebar to see all companies assigned to you. By default the list shows your active assignments — companies you still need to visit. Use the filter tabs to view completed or lost assignments as well.

Company Detail Page

Click a company name to open its detail page. This page shows the company's contact information, your outreach history, and buttons to log a contact attempt or visit.

Assignment Status

StatusMeaning
ActiveAssigned to you — outreach is in progress
CompletedYou successfully visited this company
LostThree contact attempts were made with no response
Note: A company is automatically marked Lost after you log three contact attempts without a completed visit. This frees the company up for re-assignment and keeps your list focused.

4. Logging a Contact Attempt

A contact attempt is any outreach you make to a company that does not result in a completed in-person visit. Log one each time you call, email, or stop by and do not connect with anyone.

The contact attempt form is built directly into the company detail page (see Figure 3 above). Select the method — Phone, Email, or In-Person — add any notes, and click Log Attempt.

Tip: Log a contact attempt even when you leave a voicemail or send an email with no reply. Accurate records help staff understand which companies may need a different approach.
Important: After your third contact attempt on a company, the assignment will automatically be marked Lost. Make sure your attempts are genuine; do not log them all at once just to clear a company from your list.

5. Logging a Completed Visit

A visit is an in-person meeting where you connected with someone at the company and had a meaningful conversation. This is the primary goal of the program. Click Log a Visit on the company detail page to open the visit form.

What to Fill In

FieldWhat to Capture
Contact informationConfirm or update the primary contact; note who you actually spoke with
Visit notesHow the meeting went, general tone of the conversation
WorkforceCurrent employee count, hiring or layoff status, jobs added or lost
FacilityBuilding size and whether they are at capacity
Expansion signalsAny plans to add space, equipment, or capital investment
Volunteer impactWhether you helped the company with a problem during this visit
Note: Once saved, the assignment is automatically marked Completed and the EDAWN staff team is notified. You can edit a visit note after saving if you need to add or correct information.
Tip: Fill in your visit notes as soon as possible after the meeting while details are fresh. Even brief notes are more useful than none.

6. Browsing & Requesting Companies

If you are ready to take on more work, you can browse unassigned companies and request to be assigned one. Click Browse Companies in the sidebar.

Request Rules

RuleDetail
Pending request limitYou may have up to 3 pending requests at one time
Active assignment limitNon-BBV volunteers may only hold 1 active assignment at a time. Complete your current company before requesting another (unless you are BBV certified).
Approval requiredRequests are reviewed and approved by EDAWN staff — assignment is not immediate
CompetitionMultiple volunteers can request the same company. When staff approves one, all other requests for that company are automatically denied.
BBV Certification: Once you have visited at least one company in each of three consecutive calendar months, you become eligible for BBV certification. BBV-certified volunteers can hold more than one active assignment at a time. Ask staff for details.

7. Badges & the Leaderboard

The portal tracks your activity and automatically awards badges when you hit milestones. Click Badges in the sidebar to see what you have earned and what you are working toward.

The Leaderboard ranks all volunteers by the number of completed visits and updates in real time.

Tip: Badges and leaderboard rankings are based on completed visits, not contact attempts. The best way to climb the leaderboard is to convert your outreach into actual in-person meetings.

8. Messages

The Messages section is a shared communication board for all volunteers and EDAWN staff.

Group Messages

Group messages are visible to all volunteers. Staff may use this board to share program updates, tips, or recognition. You can post your own messages as well.

Private Messages

If you need to reach EDAWN staff directly, select "Send as private message to admin" when composing a new message. Only you and staff will be able to see it.

Replies

You can reply to any message thread. Click the message title to open the thread and add your reply at the bottom.

9. Resources

The Resources page contains reference materials curated by the EDAWN team — links to documents, guides, and tools useful for your outreach work. Materials are organized by category. Click any title to open the linked resource.
